QuickEats Technologies

QuickEats Technologies is a book-built issue of ₹4,500 crores. The issue consists of a fresh issue of ₹3,000 crores and an offer for sale (OFS) of ₹1,500 crores. QuickEats Technologies opens for subscription on April 10, 2025, and closes on April 15, 2025. The allotment for QuickEats Technologies is expected to be finalized on April 20, 2025. The listing is set for NSE and BSE, with a tentative date of April 25, 2025. QuickEats price band is set at ₹85 to ₹100 per share. The minimum lot size for an application is 150 shares. The minimum investment required by retail investors is ₹12,750 (assuming the lower price band of ₹85). However, to avoid potential oversubscription issues, investors are advised to bid at the cutoff price, requiring ₹15,000 (assuming the higher price band of ₹100). The minimum lot size investment for High Net-worth Individuals (HNI) is two lots (300 shares), amounting to ₹25,500 - ₹30,000.

About QuickEats:

QuickEats Technologies is a leading player in the online food delivery and quick commerce industry, offering seamless, AI-driven solutions for customers and businesses. Founded in 2018, QuickEats has revolutionized the food-tech landscape by integrating machine learning, predictive demand analytics, and an extensive delivery network.Read more

The company offers a diverse range of services, including real-time food ordering, instant grocery delivery, and restaurant partnerships. QuickEats differentiates itself with its AI-powered recommendation system, which ensures personalized suggestions for users, optimizing order fulfillment and delivery efficiency. QuickEats has strategically expanded into hyperlocal delivery, leveraging a fleet of electric bikes and autonomous delivery bots in select cities. The company also introduced a premium subscription service, QuickEats+, which provides unlimited free deliveries, exclusive deals, and priority support.

As of June 30, 2024, QuickEats had a selection of approximately 22,000 SKUs of grocery and household items. These products include:

  • Daily essentials such as milk, bread, eggs, fruits, and vegetables.
  • On-demand cravings like snacks, ready-to-eat meals, and beverages.
  • Subscription-based purchases, including monthly or weekly essentials such as household cleaning products, toiletries, and packaged staples.
  • Emergency and convenience items, including personal care, pharmaceuticals, and stationery.
  • Festive and event-based shopping, offering sweets, party supplies, and exclusive sports event merchandise.

By September 10, 2024, QuickEats had expanded to 685 Active Dark Stores across 50 cities. These strategically located dark stores enable QuickEats to fulfill on-demand grocery deliveries within 10-20 minutes, positioning it as a leader in the quick commerce industry.

As of June 30, 2024, QuickEats employed 6,200+ employees, with a delivery partner network exceeding 200,000 across India. The company continues to scale its operations by investing in AI-driven logistics, predictive inventory management, and electric vehicle adoption to ensure faster, more sustainable deliveries.
